Subject: AUH Second Deployment — Experiment Design & Recommended Splits

Hi [partners],

Here's the experiment design for the AUH second deployment covering both Rewards and Non-Rewards.

ORGANIC BASELINE

Before sizing the splits, we looked at organic authorized user addition rates across the portfolio. Rewards cardholders add AUs at roughly 2.5 to 3 times the rate of Non-Rewards holders. Combined with the response data from the first Non-Rewards deployment, this gave us the baselines to size both experiments.

NON-REWARDS (~190K)

Based on observed response rates from the first deployment, we're moving the treatment/control split from 50/50 to 70/30 at the test group level. The experiment remains powered across all test groups at this reduced control size.

REWARDS (~540K)

For the arm split, we're recommending 70/30 — 70% Communication + Offer, 30% Communication Only. Treatment/control within each arm is also 70/30 at the test group level.

We tested this against conservative (25% increase over Non-Rewards observed rates) and optimistic scenarios. Under the conservative scenario, all arm-level comparisons and most test-group-level comparisons are powered. The calculator is attached with an editable baseline assumption if you'd like to run other scenarios.

The detailed breakdown of population sizes, MDEs, and minimum required samples per comparison is in the attached workbook.
