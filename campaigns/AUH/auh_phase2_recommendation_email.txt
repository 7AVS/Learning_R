Recommendation: Launch Phase 2 with a 70/30 test/control split (matching Phase 1), with separate controls per phase.

---

BACKGROUND

Phase 1 (NonRewards, ~190K) is live. Phase 2 extends AUH to the broader card portfolio (~540K deployment population).

To inform the design, I built the organic AU baseline across the full card portfolio. Key finding: Rewards card holders add Authorized Users organically at roughly 3x the rate of NonRewards holders. This supports the case for Phase 2 — there's a larger organic signal to amplify with the campaign.

The organic baseline data is attached for reference, but the experiment design below uses the Phase 1 observed rates as the starting point, stress-tested with two growth assumptions.

---

PHASE 2 DESIGN

Population: 539,620
Arms: Arm 1 (Comm+Offer, 70%) / Arm 2 (Comm Only, 30%)
Treatment/Control split: 70/30 within each arm
Channels: Web (24%) / Model (23%) / Random (53%)
Significance: 5% (one-sided)
Power: 80%

Phase 1 observed baseline rates (RR Baseline):

  Channel    Treatment RR    Control RR
  Web        0.12%           0.06%
  Model      0.19%           0.06%
  Random     0.05%           0.02%
  Total      0.10%           0.05%

Phase 2 will likely see higher response rates than Phase 1, since Rewards holders are more active organically. The question is how much higher. I stress-tested two scenarios:

Scenario A — Conservative (25% increase from Phase 1 baseline):
Assumes Phase 2 rates are 25% above Phase 1 observed rates. Under this assumption, MDEs remain within detectable range at the arm level for all comparisons. At the channel level within Arm 2 (the smaller arm), Web and Model groups are tighter but still powered for meaningful lift detection.

Scenario B — Optimistic (55% increase from Phase 1 baseline):
Assumes Phase 2 rates are 55% above Phase 1 observed rates. Higher baselines reduce variance, so MDEs improve across the board. All comparisons are comfortably powered.

Both scenarios are modeled in the attached MDE calculator. You can adjust the increase % in cell F1 to test any assumption.

---

SEPARATE CONTROLS

I recommend separate control groups for Phase 1 and Phase 2. Portfolio overlap between Rewards and NonRewards holders is only 2.9% — too small to justify a shared holdout. Independent controls keep attribution clean.

3. Arm structure — Same Comm+Offer / Comm Only as Phase 1, or different for Phase 2?
