Recommendation: Launch Phase 2 with a 70/30 test/control split, separate controls from Phase 1.

---

BACKGROUND

Phase 1 (NonRewards, ~190K) is live. Phase 2 extends AUH to the broader card portfolio (~540K deployment population).

I looked at the organic AU addition rates across the full portfolio. Rewards holders are about 3x more likely to add an AU than NonRewards holders without any campaign contact. That's one of the reasons Phase 2 makes sense — we're going after a group that already shows higher propensity for this product.

The organic data is attached separately. The experiment design below uses Phase 1 observed rates as the starting point.

---

PHASE 2 DESIGN

Population: 539,620
Arms: Arm 1 (Comm+Offer, 70%) / Arm 2 (Comm Only, 30%)
Treatment/Control split: 70/30 within each arm
Test groups: Web (24%) / Model (23%) / Random (53%)
Significance: 5% (one-sided)
Power: 80%

Since Phase 2 targets a more active segment, we expect higher response rates than Phase 1. The question is how much higher. I ran two scenarios using the Phase 1 observed rates as the starting point:

Scenario A — Conservative (25% increase over Phase 1 rates):
At the arm level, all comparisons are powered. At the test group level within Arm 2 (the smaller arm), Web and Model are tighter but still within detectable range.

Scenario B — Optimistic (55% increase over Phase 1 rates):
Higher baselines mean less noise, so MDEs improve across the board. All comparisons are comfortably powered.

Both scenarios are in the attached calculator. You can change the increase % in cell F1 to test other assumptions.

---

SEPARATE CONTROLS

Phase 1 and Phase 2 should each have their own control groups. The overlap between Rewards and NonRewards portfolios is only 2.9%, so there's no real benefit to sharing a holdout — it would just make things harder to measure.

---

DECISIONS NEEDED

1. Baseline growth assumption — 25% or 55%? This drives the expected lift targets and whether we're comfortable with the MDEs at the test group level.
2. Test group distribution — Same as Phase 1 (Web 24% / Model 23% / Random 53%), or different?
3. Arm structure — Same Comm+Offer / Comm Only as Phase 1, or different for Phase 2?
4. Timeline — April 30 still the target?
