## Significance callout (the blank space — lay terms, no symbols)
The lift is statistically significant, and it stays significant even at a strict, conservative
confidence bar — so this is not noise. Put plainly: if we ran this 20 times, we'd see the same
positive result nearly every time.

## Bottom text box (left / all clients) — value + interim + call (ONE block)
**Interim read — the results are real, the campaign isn't finished.**
Adding the Sales Modal on top of business-as-usual has already driven **~9,350 incremental
responses across both waves (May + June), all clients — about $4.4M in 5-year NIBT** (at $475
per response). The lift is statistically significant and holds even at a conservative confidence bar.
These are **not final numbers**: the campaign is still in-flight and well short of its full
response window, so we are reporting what the data shows today — not a guaranteed endpoint. The
trend is positive, and as later responses mature the value is expected to rise, not fall.
**Call: the Sales Modal is working. Keep it running to completion and prepare to scale.**

## Right-side text box (New-to-Campaign — dedicated)
**New-to-Campaign — where the modal pays off most.**
New-to-Campaign clients convert at **~50% — about 3x the rest of the book** — and it is exactly
where the Sales Modal adds the most: **+5.8pp in May, rising to +7.1pp in June** — the largest
lift anywhere in the study, and still climbing (June interim). Small segment today (~1,440
incremental responses / ~$0.7M 5-yr NIBT), but the **highest-leverage place to scale the modal next.**

## Footnote
Sales Modal = clients shown the modal. BAU = clients contacted through existing channels, not the
modal. Lift = the modal's incremental effect over business-as-usual (Sales Modal − BAU).
Conversion = PLI response, any channel. June wave still in-flight — figures interim and expected
to rise. $475 = 5-yr NIBT per incremental response.
