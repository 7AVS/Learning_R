CRV is cannibalizing
the limit increase.

Two offers run in the same online-banking space, to the same customers. The cheaper one is quietly suppressing the more valuable one. Recommendation: stop CRV on IM.

CRV ↔ PLI cannibalization analysis
The setup

Two offers. Same screen. Same customers.

CRV
~$36 / conversion

Installment / balance offer. The low-value play.

Limit increase (PLI)
~$675 / conversion

A higher credit limit. Worth ~20× a CRV sign-up.

Both run continuously in the IM channel. When both are live for a customer, they compete for the same click — and CRV gets in the way.

The finding
−1.08pp

Customers shown CRV take the limit increase 1.08 percentage points less often. Control 17.23% vs. Action 16.15%. z = 16.23, 95% CI 0.9–1.2pp — significant in 17 of 20 months.

Source: Q04 — hypothesis test on the gap
It's not timing

Not delayed. Lost.

Both programs deploy every month, so there is no quiet window for a "delayed" limit increase to land — it just meets the same CRV competition next month. A pure delay would wash out in steady state; instead the gap persists month after month. That is genuine lost volume, not deferral.

Source: Q04 persistence · Q12 (no dose-response)
The size of it
~42,400

limit-increase conversions lost to CRV — ≈ $28.6M gross at $675 each. Net of the CRV sign-ups you'd forgo, the trade is ~+$46M in the limit increase's favour.

Source: Q06 — net economics counts

It's not a product swap
72%

of the suppressed conversions are genuinely incremental — CRV pushed them to zero. Only ~28% are a CRV↔PLI swap. This isn't customers choosing one product over another; it's volume you simply lose.

Source: Q07 — substitution test
"Can't we just throttle it?"

Throttling doesn't beat stopping.

  • Show CRV less often per month? No lever — it already runs ~once a month. Nothing to trim.
  • Stop CRV after a customer's first few months? Recovers less than stopping, and only in the top deciles.
  • The suppression comes from CRV existing alongside PLI at all — not from frequency or timing.

There is no clever middle setting that saves CRV. The clean fix is to stop it in this channel.

Source: Q11 (throttling scenarios) · Q12 (concurrent, not dose)
Recommendation

Stop CRV on IM.

  • Recovers ~42,400 limit-increase conversions / ~$28.6M gross in the channel.
  • If a soft landing is required: suppress CRV only on the top 3 PLI-propensity deciles — that captures most of the value with the least disruption.
  • No frequency throttle exists; a duration throttle underperforms a full stop.
