Appendix — Channel mechanism (backup)
Campaign Performance: Credit Card Installment Trigger [CRV]
Mechanism: CRV exposure splits banner attention; PCL banner engagement declines 14.4% → 10.5%
% of overlapped clients with ≥1 banner click — by banner type and CRV arm
Interpretation
  • Attention is split, not lost: among clients viewing both banners, 27.5% click PCL vs. 35.7% when PCL is shown alone. The CRV banner competes for the same MB impression window.
  • CRV rarely substitutes outright: 1.8% of overlapped clients click CRV exclusively; 11.9% of both-viewers click both banners. CRV is not simply displacing PCL engagement on a 1-for-1 basis.
  • Banner clicks are an engagement diagnostic, not a conversion funnel. PCL conversion occurs across all channels and is not conditional on banner click — click rates (GA4, MB channel) and conversion rates are not a stacked funnel. The click decline is a mechanism signal; the NIBT impact is measured independently via Action vs. Control conversion rates.
