# Slide build request: discontinue CRV on mobile in-app — executive slide

Build ONE PowerPoint slide for **senior business stakeholders at RBC**
who need to approve discontinuing CRV. They will look at this slide for
60 seconds. The visuals must carry the message — they do not read
methodology, they do not need statistical caveats, they need a clear
recommendation supported by two simple visuals.

## Hard rules

- The title states the recommendation. DO NOT reframe, soften, or pivot
  to a methodology-first framing.
- Two plots. No fewer, no more. No additional decomposition charts,
  waterfalls, pie charts, or supporting visuals.
- No selection-bias language anywhere on the slide.
- No "statistical" or "rigor" language anywhere on the slide.
- No caveats boxes. No "considerations" panels.
- Annotations live INSIDE the plots, not in cards underneath.
- Numbers expressed in business units (conversions, clients, %), not
  in percentage-point gaps.

## Title (exact text — do not paraphrase)

   "Discontinue CRV on mobile in-app — yield exhausted, ~65K PCL conversions recoverable"

## Plot A (left half) — Over-contacting and diminishing returns

Title: "Most CRV contacts in 2026 hit already-contacted clients, and
        response collapses past the 5th touch"

COMBO CHART: bars (left Y-axis) + line (right Y-axis).
X-axis: contact frequency, 1 through 7. Channels combined (no IM/EM_IM_DO split).

Bars = share of 2026 deployments at each contact frequency.
Line = response rate at each contact frequency.

Data:
   Freq | % of 2026 deployments | response rate
   1    | 6.1%                  | 1.57%
   2    | 21.3%                 | 1.34%
   3    | 19.6%                 | 1.28%
   4    | 18.0%                 | 1.19%
   5    | 16.0%                 | 1.14%
   6    | 13.1%                 | 1.00%
   7    | 5.8%                  | 0.31%

Annotations IN THE PLOT:
   - Above the freq-1 bar: "Only 6% are new clients"
   - Floating label between the freq-2 to freq-6 bars: "94% are repeat contacts"
   - On the line between freq=5 and freq=7: "Response collapses past 5 contacts"

Recommended visual emphasis: subtle shading or color change on the bars
from freq=6 onward to suggest the "saturation zone" where additional
contacts produce near-zero response.

## Plot B (right half) — Cannibalization on PCL

Title: "PCL conversion is lower when clients are also exposed to CRV —
        ~65K conversions recoverable by discontinuing CRV"

LINE CHART: exactly TWO lines (do not add a third line).
X-axis: month, Oct 2024 through April 2026 (19 months).
Y-axis: PCL response rate, % scale.

Two lines, labeled inline at the right end of each line (not in a legend):
   "CRV-exposed PCL clients"           (lower line, ~14% avg)
   "Comparable PCL clients (no CRV)"   (upper line, ~15% avg)

**Shade the area between the two lines.** Use a single subtle fill color.
Label the shaded region directly inside the chart:
   "~65K PCL conversions recoverable if CRV discontinued"

Do not add a third "no_overlap" line. Do not break out the components
of the gap. The shaded area IS the message.

## Single footer line (one row, bottom of slide)

   "Source: campaigns/CRV/crv_pcl_overlap_summary.sql, campaigns/CRV/crv_contact_frequency.sql.
    Window: Oct 2024 – Apr 2026. Channel: IM (mobile in-app). 7.4M CRV-exposed PCL events."

## Style

- Clean, minimal, two plots side by side, balanced visual weight.
- Light grid only. Plot titles in one line each.
- Numbers with thousands separators (M/K abbreviations fine on labels).
- Use ~ only on the 65K estimate. Other numbers are direct measurements.
- No icons, no decorative graphics, no logos.
- Reasonable colour palette (RBC blue, dark grey, single accent for the
  shaded gap). Do not use neon, gradient, or 3D effects.

## What to refuse if asked to add it

- A bias-decomposition waterfall
- A "considerations" or "caveats" section
- A "next steps" bullet list
- A methodology note
- An additional CRV-side-impact chart
- More than two plots

The slide makes one argument with two pieces of evidence. That is all.
