# Slide build request: discontinue CRV on the mobile in-app banner

Build ONE PowerPoint slide for an internal RBC Cards-pod audience. Two
visualizations, one callout, clean and not busy. The receiving LLM must
not over-decorate — minimal text, descriptive plot titles, no extra
embellishments.

## Audience and context

Reader: Marketing Analytics partners and Cards-pod stakeholders at RBC.
They understand campaign measurement, response rates, Action/Control
design. Do not define basics. Be direct. No hedge language ("under
review", "preliminary", "being validated"). No AI flourishing. Write
like a senior analyst.

## The decision the slide supports

Discontinue CRV on the mobile in-app (IM) channel. Two converging lines
of evidence support the recommendation:

1. CRV is past saturation on its own funnel — additional contacts no
   longer produce response. Yield collapses past the 5th contact, and
   only 6% of 2026 deployments touch a new client.

2. CRV cannibalizes PCL response when both run on IM. Even after
   correcting for selection bias using CRV's own Action/Control split,
   PCL conversion drops measurably when CRV is exposed.

Both findings reinforce each other: CRV is producing less, and what
little it does produce comes at the cost of PCL.

## Headline numbers (every one must appear on the slide)

- 6%   = share of 2026 IM deployments that contact a new client (94% repeat)
- 4.4  = average prior contacts per client in 2026 (up from 2.1 in 2025)
- 0.10% = May 2026 IM response rate — effectively zero
- ~65K = PCL responders recoverable if CRV discontinued (Oct 2024 – Apr 2026)
- ~94%  = share of 2026 IM deployments going to clients already contacted at least once

Analysis window: Oct 2024 – April 2026 (May 2026 excluded from comparisons
because it is a partial month at end-of-window and the response data has
not matured).

## Style rules

- Every number on the slide must trace to a source SQL file in the footer.
- No external benchmarks, industry comparisons, or "studies show" claims.
- No emojis.
- Numbers with thousands separators (or M/K abbreviations); use ~ only on
  estimates (65K is an estimate; the percentages are direct measurements).
- Percentage points written "pp"; percent written "%".
- Two plots only. No bonus pie charts, no decorative icons, no logos.
- Light grid only. No chartjunk. Reasonable colour palette (RBC blue or
  default Office palette is fine; do not use neon).
- Plot titles can be one full line each (descriptive); slide-level text
  outside titles + footer + 3 callouts should total under 30 words.

## What NOT to include on the slide

- No methodology beyond the footer's date window and channel filter.
- No mention of the 290K naive-estimate figure (superseded by 65K
  selection-corrected number).
- No speculation about why selection bias is so large.
- No "next steps" bullets. The recommendation is in the title.
- No data tables. Plots only.
- No animation, no build sequences. Static slide.

## Recommendation framing

The title states the recommendation directly. Do not soften it. If the
receiving LLM tries to add a "considerations" or "caveats" box, reject —
caveats live in the analyst's notes, not the exec slide.
