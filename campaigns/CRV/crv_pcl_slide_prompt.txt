# Slide build request: CRV cannibalizing PCL on mobile in-app banner

Build ONE PowerPoint slide for an internal RBC Cards-pod audience that
tells the cannibalization story below. Mostly visual, minimal copy.

## Audience and context

Reader: Marketing Analytics partners and Cards-pod stakeholders at RBC.
They understand campaign measurement, response rates, Action/Control
design. Do NOT define basics. Be direct. No hedge language ("under review",
"preliminary", "validating"). No AI flourishing. Write like a senior
analyst would, plain and precise.

## The story to tell, in narrative order

1. PROBLEM. CRV and PCL both deploy to the mobile in-app banner (IM
   channel). They share audience. We need to know whether CRV
   cannibalizes PCL conversion on that shared surface.

2. SETUP. We pulled 20 months of PCL deployments on the IM channel
   (Oct 2024 - May 2026) and identified, per PCL client, whether they
   also had a CRV deployment with overlapping treatment window. We split
   the PCL universe into three groups using CRV's Action/Control design
   as a natural experiment:
     - overlap_action: PCL client + CRV Action overlap (CRV-eligible AND exposed)
     - overlap_control: PCL client + CRV Control overlap (CRV-eligible, randomly held out, NOT exposed)
     - no_overlap: PCL client, no CRV deployment at all
   The Action-vs-Control comparison isolates the CRV exposure effect from
   selection bias (CRV-eligibility is itself correlated with lower PCL
   conversion, so action-vs-no_overlap overstates the cannibalization).

3. FINDING. Cannibalization is real but smaller than a naive comparison
   suggests. Selection bias accounts for most of the original gap.
     - 67% of PCL-IM clients also got CRV-IM (7.4M of 11.0M leads over 20 months).
     - Aggregated response rates: Action 14.4%, Control 14.7%, No-overlap 18.4%.
     - True cannibalization (Action vs Control): ~0.85 pp average per month.
     - Selection bias (Control vs No-overlap): ~3.5 pp average per month.
     - Combined effect (Action vs No-overlap, naive): ~4.3 pp.
     - Direction is monotonic and consistent in 20 of 20 months.

4. COST. ~65,000 PCL responders lost to CRV cannibalization over the
   20-month window. Roughly 3,000 PCL conversions per month.

5. RECOMMENDATION. Discontinuing CRV recovers ~65K PCL conversions over
   20 months. Whether to discontinue depends on whether CRV itself
   produces more than 65K incremental conversions over the same period on
   IM. That CRV-side number is the next analysis to lock down before
   making the call.

## Slide layout

- TITLE BAR (one line). Use this exact title or a tighter equivalent:
    "CRV cannibalizes PCL on the mobile in-app banner — but selection bias inflates the headline"

- HEADLINE NUMBER CALLOUTS (a row of 3-4 small KPI tiles near the top):
    Tile 1: 67% of PCL-IM clients also exposed to CRV
    Tile 2: 0.85 pp average PCL response rate lost to CRV (Action vs Control)
    Tile 3: ~65K PCL conversions lost over 20 months
    Tile 4: 20 of 20 months show consistent cannibalization

- MAIN CHART (center-left, large):
    Line chart, 20 months on x-axis (Oct 2024 - May 2026).
    Three lines on the y-axis (PCL response rate, % scale):
      - overlap_action_response_rate (label: "PCL response — CRV-exposed clients")
      - overlap_control_response_rate (label: "PCL response — CRV-eligible held-out clients")
      - no_overlap_response_rate (label: "PCL response — clients not in CRV")
    Chart title: "PCL response rate by CRV exposure status, monthly"
    Lines should be clearly distinguishable. Action and Control will track
    close together (both CRV-eligible); No-overlap will sit ~3-4 pp above.

- SUPPORTING VISUAL (right side, smaller):
    Stacked bar or annotated number showing how the ~4.3 pp combined gap
    decomposes:
      - 0.85 pp cannibalization (the actionable piece)
      - 3.5 pp selection bias (NOT addressable by discontinuing CRV)
    Label clearly so reader sees that the actionable lift is the smaller
    of the two components.

- FOOTER ROW (bottom, plain text):
    "Source: campaigns/CRV/crv_pcl_overlap_summary.sql section E.
     Period: Oct 2024 - May 2026. Channel: IM (mobile in-app banner).
     Cohort: 11.0M PCL-IM events, 7.4M with CRV Action overlap, 385K with CRV Control overlap.
     Recommendation: pair this with CRV-side conversion volume to decide on discontinue."

## Style rules

- Cite the source SQL file in the footer.
- Every number on the slide must be traceable to the source file.
- No external benchmarks, industry comparisons, or "studies show" claims.
- No emojis.
- Numbers formatted with thousands separators (7,400,000 or 7.4M, not 7400000).
- Percentage points written as "pp", percent written as "%".
- No "approximately" / "around" hedge on numbers we have precisely — use ~ only for the implied-cost figure (~65K) since that is a model estimate, not a direct count.
- Do not put two charts side by side at equal weight — main line chart is the hero, decomposition visual is supporting.
- Keep total slide copy under 100 words outside the chart labels and footer.

## What NOT to include

- Do NOT present the discontinue-CRV recommendation as final. Frame it as conditional on CRV-side analysis still to come.
- Do NOT include the section-D-only naive estimate of 290K lost conversions. That number was inflated by selection bias and is no longer the working figure.
- Do NOT speculate about why selection bias is so large (e.g., "high-balance clients", "engaged segment") — we have not measured it.
- Do NOT include methodology beyond what is needed to read the chart. The audience can ask if they want detail.
