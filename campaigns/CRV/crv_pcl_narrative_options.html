BACK-POCKET — mechanism & robustness (only if challenged) Supporting detail for follow-up questions; not part of any option's primary narrative.
Back-pocket slide (common to all options)
Campaign Performance: Credit Card Installment Trigger [CRV]
Mechanism: co-exposure splits banner attention; result robust to sequencing and overlap duration
Banner click rate by banner type and CRV arm (GA4, MB, ~3-month window)
  • Total banner engagement falls (14.4% → 12.4%) — attention is destroyed, not reallocated, mirroring the −0.8pp total-conversion result.
  • Among clients viewing both banners, 27.5% click PCL vs 35.7% when PCL shown alone.
Robustness checks
  • Sequencing: gap 1.05pp (CRV arrives first) vs 0.95pp (PCL first) — arrival order is immaterial to the direction or approximate magnitude of the effect.
  • Overlap duration: lift is negative in every duration bucket (−0.9 to −1.1pp across 31–90 days) — the effect is not driven by short or incidental co-exposure.
  • Randomization integrity: control CRV-view contamination 6 of 55,155 (0.011%) — the control group is clean; the gap is not an artifact of spillover.
  • Source independence: click and conversion data come from different sources and different windows (GA4 single MB placement ~3 mo vs campaign response 12 mo). Compared within metric only, never across.
  • Floor is conservative: the ≈42K / ≈$30MM figure is computed on the randomized CRV control holdout — the cleanest possible causal estimate, no modelling assumptions.
