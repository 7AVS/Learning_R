Alternatives testedConfidential · Cards Pod
Finding Q11 · throttling scenariosPartial

Frequency capping cannot recover the loss — only discontinuation does.

CRV already runs ~once per customer per month, so a per-month frequency cap removes nothing. A duration cap only meaningfully reduces overlap by approximating a full stop. There is no middle setting that preserves CRV value.

Method noteConfidential · Cards Pod
Finding Q10 · delay analysisRetired

A delay-versus-block test is not identifiable under continuous redeployment.

Because both programs redeploy on a weekly cadence, a "delayed" conversion simply re-enters the same competition — there is no quiet window in which to observe recovery. The delay objection is instead answered by Q04's persistent month-over-month gap. Query retired.

Retired 2026-06-01. Superseded by Q04 (persistence) and Q12 (dose-response).11
RecommendationConfidential · Cards Pod
Recommendation

Discontinue CRV on the mobile in-app banner.

  • Recovers ~42,400 limit-increase conversions (~$28.6M gross) in the channel; ~+$46.2M net of CRV forgone.
  • If a phased exit is required, suppress CRV only on the top three PLI-propensity deciles — the bulk of the value, minimal disruption to CRV elsewhere.
  • No frequency throttle exists; a duration throttle underperforms a full stop.
Synthesis of Q04, Q06, Q07, Q08, Q11. Net figure provisional — see open items.12
