WHAT WE'RE TESTING

Whether DM drives incremental credit card acquisitions for clients in the 7th model score decile and below. These are clients who currently receive digital-only contact. The test adds DM to measure if it lifts approval rates above what we see organically.

We have a cap of 120,000 DMs per monthly deployment, which limits how many deciles we can include and how large the control group can be.

---

OPTIONS

The trade-off is straightforward: more deciles = more clients included, but we need a larger control group to fit within the 120K cap. Larger control = fewer DMs sent, but better ability to detect smaller lifts.

Option A — Deciles 7-8, 5% control
  Population:  ~99,000
  DMs sent:    ~94,000 (within cap)
  Control:     ~5,000
  MDE:         0.19pp

Option B — Deciles 7-9, 30% control
  Population:  ~158,000
  DMs sent:    ~111,000 (within cap)
  Control:     ~47,000
  MDE:         0.08pp

Option C — Deciles 7-10, 50% control
  Population:  ~126,000
  DMs sent:    ~63,000 (within cap)
  Control:     ~63,000
  MDE:         0.05pp

All three options can detect the expected DM lift based on historical rates. Option A maximizes DMs sent. Option B and C give stronger measurement but hold back more clients from treatment.

Note: lower deciles have lower baseline approval rates, so the expected lift is smaller in those groups. Expanding beyond 7-8 gives broader coverage but each additional decile contributes less.
