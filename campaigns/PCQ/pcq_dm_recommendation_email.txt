CONTEXT

The original ask was to expand DM to the 7th model score decile. When we sized the population, the 7th decile is only about 55,000 clients. Even with a minimal control holdout (5%), that's roughly 52,000 DMs per deployment — well short of our 120,000 monthly capacity.

That leaves about 68,000 DMs on the table every month.

Rather than underuse the budget, we looked at what happens if we extend into additional deciles. Below are the scenarios we modeled, all within the 120K cap.

---

OPTIONS

As we include more deciles, the total population grows beyond 120K, so we need to hold back a larger control group to stay within cap. More control means fewer DMs sent, but it also means we can detect smaller effects — which matters because lower deciles have lower response rates.

Option A — Deciles 7-8, 5% control
  Population:  ~99,000
  DMs sent:    ~94,000
  Control:     ~5,000
  MDE:         0.19pp

Option B — Deciles 7-9, 30% control
  Population:  ~158,000
  DMs sent:    ~111,000
  Control:     ~47,000
  MDE:         0.08pp

Option C — Deciles 7-10, 50% control
  Population:  ~126,000
  DMs sent:    ~63,000
  Control:     ~63,000
  MDE:         0.05pp

Option A maximizes the number of DMs sent. Options B and C cover more deciles and give us stronger measurement, but hold back more clients.

Lower deciles have lower baseline approval rates, so the expected response drops as we go further. Each additional decile contributes less, but it's still incremental reach we wouldn't have otherwise.
